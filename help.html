Frequently Asked Questions

What documents do I need to bring for tax preparation? +

Income Documents: W-2 forms, 1099 forms, business income records, unemployment statements

Deductions: Mortgage interest statements, property tax records, charitable donations, medical expenses, education expenses

Personal Info: Social Security cards, birth certificates for new dependents, bank account info, copy of last year's return

When should I file my taxes? +

The federal deadline is typically April 15th. We recommend filing early to get your refund faster, protect against identity theft, and avoid the last-minute rush. You can file as early as January when the IRS begins accepting returns.

How much do your services cost? +

Our fees vary based on the complexity of your tax situation. We provide transparent, fair pricing with no hidden fees. We'll give you a clear estimate before we begin work. Contact us for a free consultation and pricing quote tailored to your specific needs.

Can you help if I'm being audited? +

Yes! We provide full audit support and representation. We'll communicate directly with tax authorities, organize documentation, explain the process, and represent you throughout. For returns we prepared, audit support is included at no additional charge.

When will I receive my refund? +

E-filed returns with direct deposit typically take 7-21 days. E-filed with paper check takes 3-4 weeks. Paper filed returns take 6-8 weeks. We e-file all returns when possible and recommend direct deposit for fastest processing.

Do you prepare taxes for other states? +

Yes! We prepare returns for all 50 states. Whether you moved during the year, have income from multiple states, or need multi-state business filings, we can handle it. We stay current with tax laws in all states.
