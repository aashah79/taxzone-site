Frequently Asked Questions

When should I file my taxes? +

The federal tax deadline is typically April 15th each year (or the next business day if April 15th falls on a weekend or holiday). However, you can file as early as January when the IRS begins accepting returns. We recommend filing early to:

  • Get your refund faster
  • Protect against identity theft
  • Have more time to plan if you owe taxes
  • Avoid the last-minute rush

If you need more time, we can help you file an extension, which gives you until October 15th to file (but any taxes owed are still due by April 15th).

What documents do I need to bring for tax preparation? +

Income Documents:

  • W-2 forms from all employers
  • 1099 forms (interest, dividends, retirement distributions, freelance income)
  • Business income records (if self-employed)
  • Unemployment compensation statements

Deduction Documentation:

  • Mortgage interest statements (1098)
  • Property tax records
  • Charitable donation receipts
  • Medical expenses over 7.5% of income
  • Education expenses (1098-T, receipts)
  • Business expense records

Personal Information:

  • Social Security cards for all family members
  • Birth certificates for new dependents
  • Bank account information for direct deposit/withdrawal
  • Copy of last year's tax return

Can you help if I'm being audited by the IRS? +

Yes! We provide full audit support and representation for all clients. If the IRS or state tax agency selects your return for review, we'll:

  • Communicate directly with tax authorities on your behalf
  • Gather and organize required documentation
  • Explain the audit process and your rights
  • Represent you throughout the entire process
  • Help resolve any issues that arise

For returns we prepared, audit support is included at no additional charge. We stand behind our work and will handle any issues that arise from our preparation.

Do you prepare taxes for other states? +

Yes! We prepare returns for all 50 states. Whether you moved during the year, have income from multiple states, or need to file in several states due to business activities, we can handle it.

Common multi-state situations we handle include:

  • Relocating to Illinois from another state (or vice versa)
  • Working remotely for an out-of-state employer
  • Owning rental property in different states
  • Multi-state business operations
  • Retirement income subject to different state rules

We stay current with tax laws in all states to ensure compliance and minimize your overall tax burden.

When will I receive my refund? +

Refund timing depends on several factors:

  • E-filed with direct deposit: 7-21 days (fastest option)
  • E-filed with paper check: 3-4 weeks
  • Paper filed: 6-8 weeks

We e-file all returns when possible and recommend direct deposit for fastest processing. The IRS "Where's My Refund" tool provides real-time status updates once your return is accepted.

Factors that may delay refunds:

  • Early filing (before all tax documents are issued)
  • Claiming Earned Income Credit or Child Tax Credit
  • Errors or missing information
  • Identity verification requirements
